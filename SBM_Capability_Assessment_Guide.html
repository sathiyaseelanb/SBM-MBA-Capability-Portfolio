Section 01

Why This Assessment Matters

The Capability Assessment is not an exam. It is a structured mirror — a way to see yourself as a professional in formation, understand where you stand today, and plan deliberately for where you need to be by the time a recruiter asks you to prove it.

🎯
See Your Starting Point Clearly
Most students overestimate some competencies and underestimate others. The assessment surfaces both — giving you an accurate baseline before the programme shapes you.
📈
Track Real Growth Over Time
By taking the assessment at Entry, Mid-Point, and Exit, you can demonstrate measurable development — not just claim it. Growth evidence is powerful in interviews.
🗺️
Focus Your Development Effort
Your weakest competencies for your target role are your most urgent development priorities. The assessment tells you exactly which domains need attention before placement.
💼
Prepare for Recruiter Conversations
Executive recruiters ask competency questions. Knowing your own profile — and being able to articulate it with evidence — is a significant interview advantage.
🤝
Guide Your Mentoring
Your assessment results are shared with your mentor before each session — so mentoring time is spent on targeted development, not general conversation.
🏛️
Strengthen SBM's Accreditation Evidence
Your responses (anonymised and aggregated) contribute to SBM's Programme Outcome attainment data — required for NAAC and NBA accreditation cycles.
Section 02

Three Assessments Across Your MBA Journey

The assessment is taken three times — at the beginning, middle, and end of your programme. Each stage has a distinct purpose and a specific set of actions you are expected to take with your results.

Across 6 Trimesters → T1 ★ Assess T2 Develop T3 ★ Assess T4 Deepen T5 Refine T6 ★ Assess
1
Trimester 1 · Week 2–3
Entry Assessment — Establish Your Baseline
~20 mins

This is your starting point. You are not expected to score Proficient or Exemplary across the board — you are expected to be honest. The Entry Assessment is not evaluated as part of your academic grade; it is a diagnostic tool that belongs to you and your mentor.

What you will do
  • Rate yourself on all 40 competencies across the eight domains using the four-band scale (E / D / P / X). Read each band descriptor carefully — do not rate from intuition alone.
  • Identify your two or three strongest domains — these are your early confidence anchors. Note which specific competencies you can already evidence, even if informally.
  • Flag your most critical gaps for your target role or specialisation. These become the focus of your first CEE mentor conversation in Week 4–5.
  • Save your results to your Portfolio Tracker (Template B in your Student Guide). You will need them for comparison at Stage 2.
Honest self-rating No grade attached Diagnostic only Mentor review in T1 Week 4 Baseline for growth tracking
2
Trimester 3 · Week 1–2
Mid-Point Assessment — Measure Your Development
~20 mins

By Trimester 3, you have completed approximately half your programme and at least one internship exposure. This is when the assessment becomes a genuine development conversation — comparing where you are now with where you started, and recalibrating your effort for the second half.

What you will do
  • Re-rate all 40 competencies using the same four-band scale. Do not look at your Entry scores first — complete the re-rating independently, then compare.
  • Identify genuine movement — competencies where you have moved from E to D, or D to P. For each movement, name the specific experience (assessment, project, internship) that drove it. If you cannot name it, the movement may not be real.
  • Confront stalled competencies — domains that have not moved since Entry. These may indicate that coursework is not providing sufficient application opportunity, and you may need to seek co-curricular or internship exposure.
  • Refine your Gap Analysis (Template C). Update your development plan with specific actions for T4–T6 based on your target role requirements.
  • Bring a comparison report to your T3 mentor session — Entry vs Mid-Point — and ask your mentor to challenge any rating they think is inflated.
Compare with Entry baseline Evidence-backed movement only Update Gap Analysis Mentor challenge session Plan T4–T6 deliberately
3
Trimester 6 · Week 1 · Before Placement
Exit Assessment — Your Placement-Ready Profile
~25 mins

The Exit Assessment is your final capability statement before placement interactions begin. It is the most consequential of the three — not because it is evaluated, but because your recruiter conversations will test every claim you make about yourself. Your Exit profile should be defensible under detailed probing.

What you will do
  • Complete your final rating with particular rigour. For every competency you rate Proficient or Exemplary, you must be able to immediately name the PARQ artifact that evidences it. If you cannot, drop the band to Developing.
  • Produce your three-stage growth narrative — a one-page summary showing Entry → Mid-Point → Exit movement across your eight domains. This is a powerful document in a placement interview when a recruiter asks "how have you developed during your MBA?"
  • Finalise your Portfolio submission — the Exit Assessment is the last event before your mentor signs off on your Capability Portfolio. Ensure all PARQ artifacts are complete and your competency evidence is current.
  • Map your top 3–4 PARQ stories to the most likely competency interview questions for your target role. Rehearse each story verbally to your mentor before your first placement interaction.
  • Request mentor sign-off on your portfolio — a brief written endorsement from your CEE mentor that can be referenced in placement correspondence.
Every P/X rating must be evidenced Three-stage growth narrative Portfolio finalisation PARQ rehearsal before placement Mentor sign-off
Section 03

How the Assessment Works

The assessment is structured around the four proficiency bands of the SBM MBA Capability Portfolio. Understanding what each band means — and applying the descriptors honestly — is the most important skill in completing it well.

📋 Before You Begin — Checklist
I have read the full SBM MBA Capability Portfolio document and understand all eight domains
I have set aside at least 20 uninterrupted minutes — I will not rush through ratings
I have read all four proficiency band descriptors (below) before starting
I will rate based on demonstrated evidence, not on how much I have studied a topic
I understand that inflated ratings help no one — especially me in a recruiter interview
I know which stage I am completing — Entry (T1), Mid-Point (T3), or Exit (T6)
For Mid-Point and Exit: I have my previous stage results to hand for comparison
I have my Student Portfolio Guide open to Template B (Self-Assessment Tracker)

Proficiency Band Reference

Read these descriptors carefully before you begin rating. The distinction between bands is about demonstrated evidence in practice, not knowledge of concepts.

Band Code What It Means Honest Indicator
Emerging E Emerging You are aware of the competency and can define it, but your application has been limited or required significant guidance. You know about it more than you can do it. "I studied this in class and understand the concept, but I have not yet applied it independently in a meaningful context."
Developing D Developing You have applied this competency at least once, with some guidance or in a familiar, structured context. You can perform it, but not yet consistently or in novel situations. "I applied this in one or two settings — I can describe what I did — but I would still need support in a more complex or ambiguous environment."
Proficient P Proficient You apply this competency independently and consistently across different contexts. You have clear evidence — an assessment, project, or internship outcome — that a recruiter would find credible. "I can name a specific situation where I applied this independently, describe exactly what I did, and state a result. A recruiter probing me on this would be satisfied."
Exemplary X Exemplary You apply this competency with nuance and initiative beyond what is expected. You have multiple strong evidence pieces and could guide or teach others in its application. "I have multiple examples at a high level of complexity. I can speak to edge cases, limitations, and advanced applications. I have been recognised by peers or faculty for this capability."
Section 04

Eight Tips for an Accurate Self-Assessment

The quality of your assessment depends entirely on the quality of your self-honesty. These tips are drawn from the patterns CEE mentors observe most frequently in student portfolio reviews.

1
Rate evidence, not aspiration
Ask: "Can I describe a specific time I did this?" not "Do I think I am capable of this?" Capability without evidence is invisible to a recruiter — and to yourself.
2
Read each descriptor before rating
Do not rely on the band labels alone. 'Proficient' sounds good, but the descriptor is specific. Read it, apply it literally, then rate. This takes 30 extra seconds per competency and dramatically improves accuracy.
3
Distinguish knowing from doing
If you attended a lecture on Decision Trees, you know about Decision Modelling. If you built one to support an actual recommendation that changed a decision, you are developing it. The gap between these is the Emerging–Developing distinction.
4
Be especially honest about leadership
Students most commonly inflate ratings in leadership competencies — self-awareness, inclusive leadership, coaching orientation. These are the hardest to evidence and the easiest to assume. Ask: what would my teammates say if they rated me on this?
5
Gaps are information, not failure
An Emerging rating on a competency critical to your target role is your most useful result. It tells you exactly where to invest in T3–T6. A student who identifies and closes a gap deliberately is far more impressive to a recruiter than one who claims Proficient everywhere.
6
Include co-curricular evidence
Portfolio evidence is not limited to formal assessments. Club leadership, case competitions, mentoring relationships, and internship contributions all count — as long as you can describe specifically what you did and what resulted.
7
Do not compare yourself to peers
This assessment measures you against the SBM capability standard, not against your cohort. A student with three Proficient ratings and an honest gap analysis is in a stronger development position than one with 40 inflated Proficients and no idea where they need to grow.
8
Save and revisit your results immediately
Record your ratings in Template B of your Student Guide before you close the assessment window. Do not rely on memory. Your Entry ratings become your most important reference point when you sit the Mid-Point assessment in Trimester 3.
